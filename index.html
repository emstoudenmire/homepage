/ About me

I am a physics postdoc in the groups of Steven R. White and Kieron Burke at UC Irvine, where I develop and apply tensor network wavefunction methods for low-dimensional, quantum many-body systems. (The most well known method of this type is the density matrix renormalization group or DMRG.)

My interests are broad: currently I have projects ranging from density functional theory for continuum 1d electrons to simulations of strongly correlated 2d spin-lattice models. Numerical physics is exciting because it combines the best of theory and experiment: making predictions and gaining fundamental insights while observing the exact behavior of realistic systems.
